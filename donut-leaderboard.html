Submit a meeting to update the leaderboard.
Benchmark reliability: every member starts with a prior of 4 completed out of 6 expected, so initial reliability is 4 / 6 = 0.667.

Displayed reliability:
(benchmarkCompleted + completedMeetings) / (benchmarkExpected + assignedMeetings)

Meeting gain for A:
gainA = round(BASE × (1 + ALPHA × (1 - reliabilityB)))
with BASE = 20 and ALPHA = 0.75.

So meeting with someone less likely to complete meetings gives a larger boost.

    Why the benchmark helps

    Since your community has not done many meetings yet, pure completion rate would be too noisy.
    Instead of starting everybody at 0% or 100%, we use a neutral prior:

    benchmarkCompleted = 4
    benchmarkExpected = 6

    That means everybody starts near 66.7% reliability. After more meetings, real behavior gradually matters more than the benchmark.

    This is basically a simple smoothing method, so early leaderboard movement feels fair.
